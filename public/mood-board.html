Elevation & Depth

Level 0 — Flat

No shadow, no border. Used for hero bands and full-bleed sections. The canvas IS the elevation signal.

Level 1 — Hairline

1 px solid ink border. Pricing tier chrome, outline buttons. The border draws attention without lifting off the page.

Level 2 — Soft Card

Cream fill (#f8f4f0) against canvas (#fffefb). Surface-tonal elevation — no shadow needed. The warmth does the work.

Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do

  • Reserve orange (#ff4f00) for every primary CTA. The saturated orange IS the conversion signature.
  • Keep canvas WARM — #fffefb cream, not pure white. The temperature is the brand voice.
  • Set hero headlines in Degular Display (or Inter 500 substitute) at weight 500. Sentence-case, no uppercase.
  • Use 12 px radius for buttons and cards. The middle radius is the brand's signature.
  • Pair orange CTA + ink text + cream background. This three-token rhythm is the entire conversion story.

✗ Don't

  • Don't replace cream canvas with pure white. The warmth IS the brand.
  • Don't use pure black ink. The coffee-warmth in #201515 carries through every text color.
  • Don't render CTAs as pills. The brand's button is 12 px rounded rectangle — not a capsule.
  • Don't introduce a second chromatic accent. Orange + cream + coffee is the entire palette.
  • Don't substitute Degular Display with a cool geometric sans — the brand's display face has warm proportions.
